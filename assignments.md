# Assignments

This page provides an overview of the three graded assignments for the BA-Eindwerkstuk Seminar. Each assignment builds toward the final thesis and is designed to help you develop your research project incrementally.

---

## Overview

| Assignment | Weight | Due Date |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Assignment #1: Revised Research Proposal | 20% | March 13, 2025 |
| Assignment #2: Preliminary Draft | 35% | April 03, 2025 |
| Assignment #3: Empirical Draft | 35% | May 06, 2025 |
| Final Manuscript | Graded separately | June 01, 2025 |

Participation accounts for 10% of the seminar grade.

---

## Assignment #1: Revised Research Proposal

**Due:** March 13, 2025

The revised research proposal builds on the work completed in the BA2 Research Seminar. This assignment requires you to refine your research question, demonstrate engagement with the relevant literature, and outline your proposed methodology.

**Requirements:**
- Clear and focused research question
- Justification for the research topic
- Preliminary literature review
- Proposed methodology and data sources
- Timeline for completion

**Rubric:** [Assignment 1 Rubric (PDF)](rubrics/Assignment%201_rubric_BAKS%20thesis.pdf)

---

## Assignment #2: Preliminary Draft

**Due:** April 03, 2025

The preliminary draft includes a substantially developed introduction, literature review, and analytical framework/methodology section. This draft demonstrates your understanding of the existing scholarship and your approach to answering the research question.

**Requirements:**
- Introduction with clear research question and motivation
- Literature review situating your research in the field
- Analytical framework or methodology section
- Preliminary structure for body chapters

**Rubric:** [Assignment 2 Rubric (PDF)](rubrics/Assignment%202_rubric_BAKS%20thesis.pdf)

---

## Assignment #3: Empirical Draft

**Due:** May 06, 2025

The empirical draft is a near-complete version of your thesis that includes your research findings and analysis. This draft should demonstrate substantial progress toward a finished thesis.

**Requirements:**
- Complete introduction and literature review
- Fully developed methodology section
- Body chapters with research findings and analysis
- Preliminary conclusion
- Working bibliography

**Rubric:** [Assignment 3 Rubric (PDF)](rubrics/Assignment%203_rubric_BAKS%20thesis.pdf)

---

## Final Manuscript

**Due:** June 01, 2025

The final thesis manuscript is submitted to your supervisor for assessment by the first and second reader. The thesis is graded separately from the seminar.

**Requirements:**
- 10,000 words (+/- 10%)
- Formatted according to the [Style Guide](style-guide)
- Complete bibliography
- Abstract and keywords

See the [Thesis Protocol](protocol) for detailed assessment criteria.

**File naming convention:**
`LASTNAME_Firstname_studentnumber_BAKS Eindwerkstuk.docx`

---

## Submission Guidelines

- All assignments should be submitted via Brightspace by 23:59 on the due date
- Late submissions will be penalized unless prior arrangements have been made
- Use the file naming convention specified for each assignment
