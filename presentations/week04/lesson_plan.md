## Today's Session (~5 min)

## The Analytical Framework (~30 min)
- What is an analytical framework? Connects question (Week 1), literature (Week 3), and data (Week 2)
- Where it fits in the thesis structure (bridge between lit review and findings)
- Nine sections (follow along with guidelines doc on Brightspace):
  1. Introduction
  2. Theoretical Underpinnings
  3. Research Design and Rationale
  4. Sources and Selection Criteria
  5. Operationalization of Key Concepts
  6. Analytical Steps
  7. Ethical and Positional Considerations
  8. Limitations
  9. Concluding Remarks
- Walk through sections in three groups with discussion after each:
  - Foundation (1–3) → discuss: state your question, your theory, your method
  - Execution (4–6) → discuss: your sources, operationalization, steps
  - Reflection (7–9) → discuss: ethics, limitations, replicability

## Worked Example: Yemeni Refugee Media Coverage (~20 min)
- Walk through the example document (on Brightspace)
- Question, theoretical approach (Entman, Van Dijk, Korean media context)
- Coding scheme (4 frame categories), analytical steps, interpretation
- What makes it work: theory-driven, justified sources, explicit steps, connects back to RQ
- Discussion: how did the researcher get from theory to categories? What's the comparison in your project? What limitations would you flag?

## Applying This to Your Own Work (~30 min)

- Go around the group: share answers, ask questions, offer suggestions
